    Exotic dance moves

    Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question,
    my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

    Polynesian chicken dance
    Alittle known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around
    in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock or be "fruitful".

    Icelandic brownian dance
    Before the Icelandes developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which
    involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor,and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptably
    tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired
    modern styles such as Twerking.

    Arctic robot dance
    An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explores in the 1960s believed to have
    discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stiled movements, being practiced by inhabitants
    on Forthem Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this
    because they were really cold.
